How Much Is ABA Therapy With Insurance? What Families Need to Know

By ABA Therapy Billing Clarity · April 2026 · 7 min read

With insurance coverage, most families pay between $0 and $50 per ABA session through copays or coinsurance. Given that ABA therapy often involves 20-40 hours per week, the difference between paying out-of-pocket and having insurance coverage can be the difference between $150,000 and $2,000 per year.

How Insurance Reduces ABA Costs

When insurance covers ABA therapy, the insurer pays the provider directly at the contracted rate. The family's financial responsibility depends on their plan's cost-sharing structure — specifically their deductible, copay or coinsurance, and out-of-pocket maximum.

Before the Deductible

Until the annual deductible is met, the family pays the full insurance-negotiated rate for each ABA session. For a family with a $3,000 deductible and a child receiving 25 hours per week of ABA, the deductible is typically met within the first 2-4 weeks of treatment. After that, cost-sharing drops dramatically.

After the Deductible

Once the deductible is met, the family pays either a flat copay per visit (typically $20-$50) or a coinsurance percentage (typically 10-20% of the allowed amount). For a 3-hour ABA session with a negotiated rate of $180, a 20% coinsurance would be $36.

After the Out-of-Pocket Maximum

Once the family reaches their annual out-of-pocket maximum (typically $6,000-$16,000 for a family), the insurer pays 100% of covered ABA services for the remainder of the year. Because ABA is high-volume, many families hit their OOP max within the first few months of the year — meaning the rest of the year's ABA therapy is fully covered at no additional cost.

Financial planning tip: If your child needs full-time ABA, you will almost certainly hit your out-of-pocket maximum. When choosing insurance plans during open enrollment, compare the OOP max rather than the premium — a plan with a higher premium but lower OOP max may cost less overall when ABA utilization is factored in.

Cost by Insurance Type

Employer PPO/HMO Plans

These typically have the most generous ABA coverage. Copays range from $20-$50 per visit, and family OOP maximums are usually $6,000-$12,000. Most large employer plans cover ABA without annual dollar caps, though some require prior authorization for treatment beyond 25-30 hours per week.

Marketplace (ACA) Plans

ACA plans cover ABA as part of the habilitative services essential health benefit. Cost-sharing varies by metal tier — Bronze plans have higher deductibles and coinsurance, while Gold/Platinum plans have lower cost-sharing but higher premiums. OOP maximums on ACA plans are capped by federal rules.

Medicaid / CHIP

Medicaid-covered ABA typically has zero or minimal cost to families — usually $0-$5 per visit. Medicaid covers ABA in all 50 states for EPSDT-eligible children. The trade-off is that Medicaid reimbursement rates to providers are lower, which means fewer providers accept Medicaid and waitlists can be longer.

TRICARE (Military)

TRICARE covers ABA therapy through the Comprehensive Autism Care Demonstration (ACD) program. Families typically pay low copays, and TRICARE has historically been one of the more ABA-friendly payers in terms of authorized hours and covered services.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

  • Out-of-network providers — If your ABA provider is out-of-network, you may be responsible for the difference between the provider's charge and the insurer's allowed amount (balance billing). This can add hundreds per month.
  • Assessment costs — Some plans apply a separate deductible or higher cost-sharing for the initial diagnostic evaluation (which is separate from ABA) or the 97151 assessment.
  • Caregiver training (97156) — A few plans exclude or limit coverage for parent training. Verify whether 97156 is covered under your plan.
  • Authorization gaps — If your authorization expires and there's a gap before renewal, you may be responsible for the cost of sessions during the gap period.

The Bottom Line

Insurance makes ABA therapy financially accessible for the vast majority of families. The key actions are verifying benefits before starting therapy, understanding your deductible and OOP max, choosing an in-network provider, and staying on top of authorization renewals. For most families with insurance, the annual out-of-pocket cost for full-time ABA is between $3,000 and $8,000 — a fraction of the $100,000+ it would cost without coverage.
