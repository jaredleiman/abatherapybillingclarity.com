CPT Code 97151: ABA Assessment and Treatment Plan Billing Guide

By ABA Therapy Billing Clarity · April 2026 · 8 min read

CPT 97151 — Behavior Identification Assessment — is the code that initiates every ABA case. It covers the comprehensive assessment process including direct observation, caregiver interviews, standardized assessments, Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA), and treatment plan development. Getting this code right sets the foundation for every subsequent claim on the case.

What CPT 97151 Covers

97151 covers two distinct activities that are billed under the same code:

  • Behavior identification assessment — Direct observation, standardized testing (VB-MAPP, ABLLS-R, AFLS, etc.), caregiver/teacher interviews, review of records, and FBA procedures
  • Treatment plan development — Writing the individualized treatment plan based on assessment findings, including goal development, program design, and behavior intervention plan (BIP) creation

Each unit represents 15 minutes. The assessment typically requires 8-32 units (2-8 hours) depending on the complexity of the case and the payer's authorization.

Who Can Bill 97151

97151 must be billed by a qualified healthcare professional — in ABA, this means a BCBA or BCBA-D. BCaBAs may conduct portions of the assessment under BCBA supervision depending on state and payer rules, but the billing provider is the supervising BCBA. RBTs cannot independently bill 97151.

Documentation Requirements

Assessment Components

Your 97151 documentation must include a comprehensive behavioral health history, presenting concerns from caregivers and referral source, results of standardized assessments with scores and interpretations, direct observation data across relevant settings, Functional Behavior Assessment findings identifying antecedents, behaviors, and consequences, and a summary of strengths and deficits across all relevant domains.

Treatment Plan Components

The treatment plan developed under 97151 must include specific measurable behavioral objectives with baseline data, recommended hours of service per week broken down by code (97153, 97155, 97156), behavior intervention strategies for each target behavior, skill acquisition program descriptions, caregiver training goals, discharge criteria, and an estimated duration of treatment.

Critical payer requirement: Most commercial payers and Medicaid programs require the 97151 assessment and treatment plan to be submitted with the initial prior authorization request. An incomplete or poorly written treatment plan is the #1 reason initial authorizations are denied or reduced in hours.

Re-Assessment Billing

97151 is also used for periodic re-assessments — typically every 6 months. Re-assessment 97151 claims must include updated standardized assessment scores compared to baseline, progress analysis on all active treatment goals, updated FBA if behavior targets have changed, and a revised treatment plan with new or modified goals. The re-assessment is what supports your next authorization request. Weak re-assessments lead to reduced authorized hours.

Common Billing Errors

  • Billing 97151 for activities performed by an RBT — Assessment is a BCBA-level activity
  • Under-documenting the FBA — A checklist-style FBA without narrative analysis and data will be denied
  • Missing baseline data — Every treatment goal must have a documented baseline. Goals without baselines cannot demonstrate progress
  • Billing 97151 and 97155 on the same date — Some payers restrict same-day billing of assessment and direct supervision codes
  • Exceeding authorized assessment hours — Always verify how many 97151 units the payer has authorized before completing the assessment

The Bottom Line

CPT 97151 is where your clinical credibility and billing accuracy converge. A thorough, well-documented assessment with measurable goals and clear treatment recommendations does three things simultaneously: it guides clinically appropriate treatment, it secures adequate authorization from the payer, and it creates the documentation foundation that protects every subsequent claim on the case. Invest the time to get 97151 right — everything else depends on it.
