CPT Code 97156: ABA Caregiver Training Documentation Requirements

By ABA Therapy Billing Clarity · April 2026 · 7 min read

CPT 97156 — Family Adaptive Behavior Treatment Guidance — covers the time a BCBA spends training caregivers to implement behavioral strategies. It is one of the most clinically important ABA services — and one of the most under-billed. Effective caregiver training accelerates treatment progress, supports generalization of skills, and reduces problematic behaviors across settings.

What 97156 Covers

97156 is billed when a BCBA provides face-to-face training to a caregiver (parent, guardian, teacher, or other significant person in the client's life) on how to implement behavioral strategies. Billable activities include teaching caregivers to implement specific behavior reduction procedures, training on reinforcement systems and prompting strategies, coaching on how to facilitate skill generalization at home and in community settings, reviewing behavioral data with caregivers and adjusting home strategies accordingly, and training on crisis prevention and management techniques.

Each unit represents 15 minutes of face-to-face time with the caregiver. The client does not need to be present — in fact, many 97156 sessions are more effective when conducted with the caregiver alone so the focus is entirely on training.

Who Qualifies as a "Caregiver"

97156 can be billed for training provided to parents or legal guardians, grandparents or other family members who regularly care for the client, foster parents, teachers and school staff (with payer-specific limitations), daycare providers, and any individual identified in the treatment plan as a significant caregiver. The key requirement is that the person being trained has a role in the client's daily life and will implement the behavioral strategies being taught.

Documentation Requirements

97156 session notes must include the date, start and end times, and total duration, who was trained (relationship to client), which treatment plan goals the training addresses, what specific strategies or techniques were taught, how the caregiver demonstrated competency (role play, practice, return demonstration), the caregiver's performance feedback, and next steps for the caregiver to implement before the next session.

Documentation best practice: The strongest 97156 notes include a competency measure — for example, "Parent demonstrated correct implementation of 3-step prompting procedure for manding with 90% fidelity across 5 practice trials." This connects the training to measurable outcomes and supports medical necessity.

97156 vs. 97155: The Critical Distinction

The most common coding error with 97156 is confusing it with 97155. The rule is simple: if the BCBA's primary activity is directing or modifying the client's treatment while the client is present, that's 97155. If the primary activity is training the caregiver, even if the client happens to be in the room, that's 97156. The focus of the session determines the code, not the physical presence of the client.

Payer Coverage Considerations

While most payers cover 97156, there are important variations. Some payers limit the total units of caregiver training authorized per authorization period. A few payers require the client to be present during caregiver training. Some Medicaid MCOs have specific requirements about who qualifies as a caregiver for billing purposes. Telehealth delivery of 97156 is widely accepted but verify with each payer.

Common Billing Errors

  • Under-billing 97156 — Many practices provide informal caregiver guidance during 97155 supervision sessions but don't bill the caregiver training component separately. If you're spending 15+ minutes training the parent, that's billable under 97156.
  • Billing 97156 for phone calls — Unless the payer specifically allows telephonic or telehealth 97156, phone-based caregiver guidance is typically not billable.
  • Missing training goals in the treatment plan — 97156 must connect to caregiver training goals in the treatment plan. If no parent training goals exist, the claim has no documentation anchor.
  • Billing 97156 and 97155 simultaneously — A BCBA cannot bill both codes at the same time. If the session transitions from client supervision to caregiver training, split the time and bill each code for its respective portion.

The Bottom Line

Caregiver training is where ABA treatment gains its real-world impact. Children who have trained caregivers implementing strategies at home make faster progress and maintain gains better than those who receive clinic-only treatment. For practices, 97156 represents both a clinical imperative and a revenue opportunity that is frequently under-utilized. Build caregiver training goals into every treatment plan and bill for the training you're already providing.
