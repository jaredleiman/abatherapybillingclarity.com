CPT Code 97155: BCBA Direct Supervision Billing Requirements

By ABA Therapy Billing Clarity · April 2026 · 8 min read

CPT 97155 — Adaptive Behavior Treatment with Protocol Modification — is the code for BCBA direct supervision and treatment modification during ABA sessions. It covers the time a BCBA spends directly observing treatment, modeling techniques for the RBT, modifying protocols based on client response, and directing the treatment session in real time.

What 97155 Covers

97155 is billed when a BCBA is present during an ABA session and is actively engaged in clinical direction. Billable activities include direct observation of the RBT implementing treatment protocols, real-time protocol modification based on client behavior, modeling correct implementation of behavioral procedures for the RBT, conducting probe trials or assessments during the session, and directing the session flow based on clinical judgment.

Each unit represents 15 minutes of BCBA face-to-face time with the client. The BCBA must be physically present (or via approved telehealth modality) with the client — reviewing notes or providing feedback to the RBT outside the client's presence is not billable under 97155.

Supervision Ratio Requirements

Most payers require a minimum percentage of BCBA supervision relative to total direct service hours. Common requirements include the BACB recommendation of supervision comprising a minimum of 5% of direct hours for experienced RBTs and up to 20% for newly credentialed technicians. Medicaid programs vary but typically require 5-10% supervision ratios. Commercial payers generally follow the BACB guidelines but may impose their own minimums.

Compliance risk: Under-supervising is an audit trigger. If your practice averages 3% supervision across cases while the payer requires 5%, every 97153 claim on those cases becomes vulnerable to denial on the grounds that the treatment was not adequately supervised.

Documentation Requirements

97155 supervision notes must include the date, start and end times, and total minutes of supervision, names of the client and the RBT being supervised, which treatment plan goals/programs were observed, specific observations about RBT fidelity to the treatment protocol, any protocol modifications made during the session with clinical rationale, competency feedback provided to the RBT, and next steps or recommendations for treatment adjustments.

97155 vs. 97156: Know the Difference

These two codes are frequently confused. The distinction is simple: 97155 is face-to-face with the client — the BCBA is observing or directing treatment. 97156 is face-to-face with the caregiver — the BCBA is training the parent or guardian. If the BCBA is in the session with the client, that's 97155. If the BCBA is meeting with the parent to teach strategies, that's 97156. Billing 97155 for parent training or billing 97156 for session observation is a coding error that will be caught in audit.

Telehealth Supervision

Many payers now allow 97155 supervision via telehealth, accelerated by post-pandemic policy changes. However, telehealth supervision rules vary significantly by payer. Key considerations include whether the payer allows synchronous video supervision for 97155, whether there are limits on the percentage of supervision that can be delivered via telehealth, whether specific telehealth modifiers are required on the claim, and whether the RBT must be physically present with the client during telehealth supervision.

Common Billing Errors

  • Billing 97155 for indirect supervision — Reviewing session data, writing supervision notes, or providing feedback via email/phone is not face-to-face and is not billable under 97155
  • Billing 97155 and 97153 simultaneously for the same provider — A BCBA cannot bill their own direct treatment as 97153 (technician code) and supervision as 97155 at the same time
  • Double-billing supervision time — If two RBTs are working with different clients and the BCBA observes both, the BCBA can only bill 97155 for one client at a time
  • Missing supervision notes — Every 97155 claim must have a corresponding supervision note. Claims without notes will be denied on audit

The Bottom Line

97155 is both a clinical necessity and a billing requirement. Adequate supervision protects treatment quality, meets payer requirements, and creates the documentation trail that supports your entire case. Practices that view supervision as overhead to be minimized are the ones that face authorization reductions and audit findings. Practices that invest in thorough, well-documented supervision build stronger cases, retain authorizations, and protect their revenue.
